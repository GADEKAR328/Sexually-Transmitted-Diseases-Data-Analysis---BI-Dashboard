# 🎯 Project Overview

Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs) continue to affect millions of people worldwide and remain a major public health challenge.

This Power BI dashboard analyzes four major sexually transmitted diseases:

- 🦠 HIV/AIDS
- 🦠 Hepatitis B
- 🦠 Human Papillomavirus (HPV)
- 🦠 Syphilis

The dashboard provides an interactive platform to explore disease prevalence, incidence rates, mortality trends, and regional disease distribution, helping healthcare professionals and policymakers make data-driven decisions.

# 📊 Numerical Analysis & Key Findings

## 🦠 HIV/AIDS

| Metric | Value |
|----------|---------|
| Highest Prevalence Region | Africa |
| Approx. Prevalence | 95 |
| Lowest Prevalence Region | Europe |
| Approx. Prevalence | 35 |

### Insight

Africa exhibits approximately **171% higher prevalence** than Europe, highlighting the continued need for targeted awareness and treatment programs.

## 🦠 Hepatitis B

| Metric | Value |
|----------|---------|
| Highest Prevalence Region | Africa |
| Approx. Prevalence | 85 |
| Lowest Prevalence Region | Oceania |
| Approx. Prevalence | 30 |
| Difference | 55 |

### Insight

Africa demonstrates nearly **2.8 times higher prevalence** than Oceania, emphasizing the importance of vaccination and early screening programs.

## 🦠 HPV

| Metric | Value |
|----------|---------|
| Highest Prevalence Region | Europe |
| Approx. Prevalence | 100 |
| Second Highest Region | Africa |
| Approx. Prevalence | 95 |
| Lowest Prevalence Region | Asia |
| Approx. Prevalence | 28 |

### Insight

Europe's prevalence is approximately **257% higher** than Asia, underlining the importance of HPV vaccination and cervical cancer prevention programs.

## 🦠 Syphilis

### Insight

Africa records over **20 times higher prevalence** than Oceania, indicating a significant geographical concentration of disease burden.

# 🔍 Major Findings

📌 Africa appears as the highest prevalence region in 3 of 4 diseases analyzed.

📌 Syphilis demonstrates the highest observed prevalence value (~102).

📌 HPV shows exceptional prevalence concentration in Europe.

📌 HIV/AIDS and Hepatitis B display encouraging declines in mortality and new cases.

📌 Significant regional disparities suggest differences in healthcare access, awareness, screening, and prevention efforts.
